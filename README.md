**Overview**

This project is an interactive Power BI dashboard built to analyze the performance of Global Superstore, a global online retailer headquartered in New York. The superstore offers a catalogue of over 10,000 products across three main categories — Office Supplies (e.g., staples), Furniture (e.g., chairs), and Technology (e.g., smartphones) — serving customers across multiple countries worldwide.

The dashboard surfaces data-driven insights into sales, profitability, shipping, customer behavior, and regional performance to support strategic business decisions.

**Objectives**

The analysis was designed to answer the following business questions:

**Top Performing Countries & Products** — Which three countries generated the highest total profit in 2014, and which three products drove the most profit in each?

**Shipping Costs in the United States** — Which three product subcategories carry the highest average shipping cost in the US?

**Nigeria's Profitability** — How does Nigeria's 2014 profit compare to other African countries, and what factors (shipping costs, discounts) explain its performance?

**Southeast Asia Subcategory Analysis** — Which product subcategory is least profitable in Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam), and which specific country is the biggest contributor to this loss?

**Least Profitable U.S. City** — Which city (with at least 10 orders) has the lowest average profit, and what drives this?

**Australia's Profitable Subcategories** — Which product subcategory yields the highest average profit in Australia?

**Customer Returns & Valuable Customers** — Who are the customers that returned items, what segments do they belong to, and who are the most valuable customers by profit contribution?

**Key Insights**

**Top 3 Countries (2014 Profit)**: ** United States (`$286K`), China, and India (`$129K`)

**Top US Subcategories**: Copiers, Machines, and Tables lead in highest-profit products

**Nigeria**: Recorded a significant loss of -$23,285.19, the lowest profitability among African countries. Contributing factors include high shipping costs ($1,518.48) and an average discount rate of 38.09% — far above peer countries

**Southeast Asia**: Tables are the least profitable subcategory, with a total loss of ~$19K. Indonesia accounts for the majority of this (~$11K), followed by the Philippines and Thailand

**Least Profitable US City**: Lancaster, with an average loss of -$157.37, driven by high discount spending ($4,869.77) and shipping costs ($1,103.70) relative to total sales ($9,891.46 across 46 orders)

**Australia**: Appliances, Copiers, and Phones are the most profitable subcategories; Tables are the least profitable

**Top Customer**: Tamara Chand, contributing the highest profit among all customers

**Recommendations**

The Store should:
Expand product range in high-performing subcategories (Appliances, Copiers, Phones) and leverage customer feedback to sustain their success

Implement cross-selling and upselling strategies to grow revenue from profitable segments

Re-evaluate the discount strategy in Nigeria and the Tables subcategory in Southeast Asia to reduce losses

Use data-driven insights for pricing, product planning, and targeted marketing

Invest in personalized marketing and customer loyalty programs to increase engagement and retention
